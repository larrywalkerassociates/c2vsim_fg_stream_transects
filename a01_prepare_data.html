Methods

Methods#

We extracted stream nodes and their corresponding simulated groundwater heads from CV2SIM-FG v1.5 [California Department of Water Resources, 2025],observed groundwater levels from CASGEM [California Department of Water Resources, 2025], and high-quality lithology logs from DWR’s Airborne Electromagnetic Survey (AEM) for areas 6 [California Department of Water Resources, 2023] and 7 [California Department of Water Resources, 2023]

We connected the stream nodes to create a Sacramento River streamline. For each node and timestep, we computed groundwater table as the head of the shallowest layer where the head exceeded the bottom elevation. Using simulated heads and CV2SIM-FG v1.5 bottom layer elevations, we calculated the groundwater table elevation for each node and timestep as the head of the highest layer where the head is above the layer bottom elevation. We aggregated AEM lithology textures into broader categories for visualization, as shown in Table 1. We included CASGEM and AEM lithology wells within 500 m (1,640 ft) of the river. Where CASGEM well labels overlapped, we prioritized observation over irrigation and domestic wells, and shallow over deep wells. When shallow observation wells overlapped, we manually adjusted the x-coordinate of the projection onto the transect for label readability.

We analyzed a 190-mile section of the Sacramento River from Red Bluff to Sacramento, which we divided in three sections for clarity. We assigned cross section mileage relative to the confluence with the San Joaquin River. The soutern transect covers from Sacramento to Yuba City (mile 50 to 120). The central transect spans from Yuba City to Chico (mile 120 to 190). The northern transect ranges from Chico to Red Bluff (mile 190 to 240).

For our workflow, we used libraries rasterio [Gillies and others, 2013], shapely [Gillies et al., 2025], pandas [McKinney, 2010], geopandas [Jordahl et al., 2020], and numpy [Harris et al., 2020] for data processing. For preparing the animations we used matplotlib [Hunter, 2007]. We handled deployment was handled using jupyter book [Executable Books Community, 2020] and GitHub Pages [GitHub, n.d.].
